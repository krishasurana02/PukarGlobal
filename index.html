Welcome To Pukar Global

OUR STORY

Established in 2019, a community service platform PUKAR Global based in Delhi NCR was set up. We wanted to make a difference, a day when no one sleeps empty stomach and possesses the right education to be independent. We analysed that we were doing no good to improve their situation and living standards of the underprivileged among our locality. It made us think a little deeper and we finally came to a conclusion. “We can't eradicate poverty by charity but by education. Unless they are taught to learn, to earn, to live, to pursue the talent they possess, we can't succeed in fulfilling their needs.” This “link” or enabling position has determined PUKAR Global’s strategic choices at every juncture – from the fundraising methods it employs, to the nature of its relationship with the people it partners with. We aim to spread blessings and fulfill wishes by linking beneficiaries with donors.

“Numbers don’t count. Even if we can change one life, it would mean a great deal to our team.”

Our mission is to empower and create awareness among underprivileged/below the poverty lines individuals, especially children, to emerge as independent and educated citizens of India.

How can I track the money I donated?

We will send you an acknowledgement as soon as we receive the donated amount. We provide continuous updates and reports of the project/ programme you choose to support.

How can I volunteer or intern with your team?

We believe that there is no age for volunteering or interning. Anybody with a passion and willingness to bring about a change in society can contact us to volunteer. We give our team members an opportunity to create/ run a project or an event, independently. Each intern is also given an experience letter along with heartfelt memories with us! Based on the internship performance we also give out minimal stipends.

OUR CAMPAIGNS



Covid - 19 Relief Campaign:

With an aim to help the underprivileged In the times of the Covid-19 pandemic when wearing the face mask is mandatory for everyone as per the guidelines of the Union health ministry, PUKAR Global organised "Gift A Mask" programme, realising that the markets were running out of masks. Those people needed all the protection against the coronavirus. Due to the shortage of masks in the market, they often had to wear the same masks beyond its allowed usage. As a community service initiative, PUKAR Global gifted masks to those people.

Clothes Distribution Campaign:

Winter is that time of the year which calls for hot coffee, snuggling up in a warm cosy blanket and staying warm in your homes. But for those who live on the streets, it is a matter of survival in the chilling temperatures. Far from hot food or warm blankets, even getting one meal a day and something to cover their body seems like a dream. But PUKAR Global is here to fulfil their dreams. Every winter, our organization organizes a warm clothes Donation Campaign for underprivileged living in the open on the streets. All the members of PUKAR Global make their best efforts to reach out to maximum underprivileged people.

Christmas Celebration Campaign:

Every Christmas (25th of December) PUKAR Global arranges Samosas, toffee, chocolate and biscuit packages for children's celebration. A special time full of joys, hopes and smiles!

Underprivileged Statistics in India

By Parul Yadav

According to recent studies, 8.4 crore Indians are poor as of 2017 down from 270 million in 2011. India is a developing country but poverty is still a big challenge. Approximately 80 Million people of India are living in extreme poverty.



Poverty in India: Stats and Data
  • Approximately 17 million children in India work due to poverty. 50% of children in India don’t go to school because of poverty.
  • India has the highest rate of child marriage in the world. Indian girls get married at an early age and spend their whole life working in home for survival.
  • Poor families often sell their children to contractors who promise productive jobs in the cities and the children end up being employed in brothels, hotels and domestic work.
  • Major states facing poverty in India are Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Jharkhand, Orissa, Chhattisgarh and West Bengal.
  • According to an analysis, India’s middle class may have shrunk by 30% due to coronavirus, while the number of poor people earning less than 150 per day.
  • More than 50% of Indian children are malnourished. Millions of children work to help their families in India because the adults do not have proper employment and income.
  • In India, less than half of children between the age 5 and 15 go to school.
  • 75% of women in India were underage at the time of their wedding.
  • More than 50% of girls in the age group of 5 to 10 years are illiterate.

    • Labor : Stats and Data 2021
  • 152 million children around the world are still in child labor, out of which 73 million are in hazardous work.
  • According to a survey by GOI (Government of India), 95% of the children in the age group of 6-13 years are attending educational institutions, the corresponding figures for those in the age group of 14-17 years are 79.6 %.
  • According to the census of India 2011, 10.1 million working children are in the age group of 5-14 years, out of whom 8.1 million are in rural areas mainly engaged in cultivation and agricultural labor.
  • UNESCO estimates based on the 2011 census, around 38.2 million children are “out of school”, which is 18.3 percent of the total children in the age group of 6-13 years.
